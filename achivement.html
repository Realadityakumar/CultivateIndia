Achievement in Agriculture during last 75 years

1.1 Agriculture, Economy and population between 1951 and 2021

India’s journey from 1950-51 to 2021-22 has been marked by significant growth. The population surged from 35.9 crore to 136.9 crore. Remarkably, food production outpaced this, increasing from 106 million tonnes to 936 million tonnes. The agriculture sector’s income saw a 7.27-fold increase, reflecting the sector’s resilience and growth. However, the non-agricultural sector outperformed with the national income rising 28.44 times. The workforce in agriculture expanded from 9.72 crore to 24.67 crore. These figures highlight India’s economic transformation over seven decades, underlining the pivotal role of both the agricultural and non-agricultural sectors in this journey. The data underscores the importance of continuous innovation and policy support in ensuring sustainable growth in the future.

  1. Population: India’s population increased from 35.9 crore to 136.9 crore, a 3.81 times increase with a compound growth rate of 1.90%.
  2. Food Production: Food production saw a substantial increase from 106 million tonnes to 936 million tonnes, an 8.83 times increase with a compound growth rate of 3.12%.
  3. Agriculture Sector Income: The income from the agriculture sector increased 7.27 times with an annual trend growth rate of 2.83%.
  4. Total Economy: The overall national income increased by 28.44 times, with an average annual growth rate of 4.83%.
  5. Agriculture Workers: The number of agriculture workers increased from 9.72 crore in 1950-51 to 24.67 crore in 2021-22, a 2.58 times increase with a compound growth rate of 1.34%.

1.2 Performance at sub sector level

  1. Diverse Growth: Agriculture’s sub-sectors, including crops, livestock, fishery, and forestry, have experienced varying growth rates1.
  2. Horticulture Rise: Horticulture, particularly fruits and vegetables, has seen significant growth since the 1960s.
  3. Livestock Success: The livestock sector, especially dairy, has consistently outperformed the crop sector in growth.
  4. Fishery Acceleration: The fishery sector has achieved the highest growth rate of 7.10% from 2005-06 to 2020-212.

1.3 Changes in composition of agriculture sector

  1. Diverse Growth: Different agriculture commodities have experienced varying growth rates, leading to changes in the sector’s composition.
  2. Technology & Market Influence: Technological advancements and market demands have significantly shaped the sector’s evolution.
  3. Declining Share of Cereals: The share of cereals in the total value of agriculture output has decreased over time.
  4. Rise of Horticulture & Livestock: There has been a notable increase in the share of fruits, vegetables, and livestock products.

1.4 Achievement in food production

  1. Initial Years Post-Independence: Food production was sufficient to supply 0.81 kg of food per person per day or 296 kg per year.
  2. Green Revolution Impact: Per capita food production increased to 1.258 kg/person/day or 459 kg/year around 2000-01.
  3. 21st Century Acceleration: A 50% increase in per capita food production was recorded in less than 25 years, marking a significant departure from the trend growth rate of the first 50 years after Independence.
  4. Future Projections: The next 50% increase in per capita food production is likely to be achieved in less than 25 years5.

1.5 Growth at State level

  1. Diverse Growth Rates: States show varied growth rates in agriculture, with some achieving high productivity and others lagging behind.
  2. Productivity vs. Growth: States with the highest land productivity do not necessarily have the highest productivity for specific crops like rice and wheat1.
  3. Demand-Driven Performance: States that have adapted their crop patterns to changing demand perform better than those with rigid crop patterns.
  4. Irrigation and Productivity: States leading in land productivity have lower irrigation coverage compared to states like Punjab and Haryana, suggesting that factors other than irrigation contribute to high productivity.

1.6 Agriculuture Trade

  1. Initial Variability: Initially, there was no clear trend in net trade, with exports and imports fluctuating due to domestic production instability and inward-looking trade policies.
  2. Post-1990 Growth: Since 1990, exports have consistently exceeded imports, driven by steady agriculture production growth, slowing population growth, and liberalization of trade policies.
  3. Export Increase: The fraction of output exported by India increased from 2.80% in the early 1990s to 6.98% in 2020-21.
  4. Undernutrition Decline: The number of undernourished persons in India declined from 261.5 million in 1979-81 to less than 200 million around 2010, with a steady drop in the prevalence of undernutrition1. However, there was some deterioration during the Covid-period.
